Abstract

Can humanoid robots lift and manipulate payloads com- parably to human’s level? More fundamentally, how can we maximize a humanoid robot’s effective “muscle” capability through whole-body coordination? Fortunately, recent ad- vances in humanoid hardware and control have enabled sub- stantial progress in loco-manipulation (cite) and bimanual manipulation (cite), allowing robots to interact with, carry, and place objects in structured settings. However, the fun- damental capability—pickup control remains underexplored (cite). Most existing approaches assume a predefined initial posture or grasp configuration, which significantly limits their applicability in real-world scenarios. Also, have not leverage the coordination of the whole-body dynamics to maximize its lifting capability. 1 We envision that unlocking robust and optimized pick-up-and-place behaviors is essen- tial for deploying humanoid robots in practical daily and industrial tasks. To this end, we investigate how humanoid robots can jointly optimize its pick-up posture and its whole- body trajectory, enabling strength-efficient and dynamically feasible payload manipulation and locomotion.
